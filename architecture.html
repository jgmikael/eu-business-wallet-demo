Six-Layer Technical Architecture

The EU Business Wallet reference architecture is structured in six distinct layers. Click each layer to explore its role and technical details.

1. Canonical Semantic Foundation

The stable, authoritative core of interoperability

2. Semantic Application Profiles

Constraints and rules for specific use cases

3. Exchange Objects & Syntax Bindings

Multiple serialization formats above one semantic core

4. Interaction & Exchange Protocols

How objects are exchanged between parties

5. Trust Services & Trusted Delivery

Cryptographic integrity and evidentiary services

6. Governance, Conformance & Audit

Lifecycle management and accountability

Key Architectural Insights

  • Layers 1-2 are stable: Semantic foundation and profiles change slowly through governance
  • Layers 3-4 are replaceable: Multiple syntaxes and protocols can coexist
  • Layer 5 is orthogonal: Trust services apply to any payload/protocol combination
  • Layer 6 spans all layers: Governance applies to semantics, formats, protocols, and trust

Why This Matters

This layered architecture enables:

  • Future-proofing: New protocols (e.g., OpenID4VC extensions) can be adopted without changing semantics
  • Legacy integration: XML-based systems can participate via semantic mapping
  • Flexibility: Wallets can use JSON-LD VCs while backend systems use eDelivery
  • Trust: Signatures, seals, and QERDS work regardless of payload format
  • Governance: Centralized semantic governance with decentralized implementation
